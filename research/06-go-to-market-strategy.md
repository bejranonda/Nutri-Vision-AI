# Go-to-Market Strategy

**Last Updated:** November 17, 2025

## Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive go-to-market (GTM) strategy for NutriVision AI, incorporating digital health startup best practices, Thai market insights, and emerging GTM models.

---

## New Digital Health GTM Models

### Emerging GTM Motions (from Andreessen Horowitz Research)

1. **B2C2B**: Acquire consumers directly, then leverage user base for enterprise contracts
2. **B2SMB**: Target small/medium businesses with faster sales cycles
3. **Risk-Based Contracting**: Outcomes-based pricing models
4. **Two-Sided Networks**: Build platforms connecting multiple stakeholders
5. **Distribution Partnerships**: Partner with aggregators for reach

**NutriVision Approach**: Hybrid B2C2B + Distribution Partnerships
- Build consumer base first (credibility)
- Use traction for corporate wellness deals
- Partner with food delivery platforms for distribution

## Phase 1: Bangkok Launch (Months 1-6)

### Target: 10,000 MAU | 400 Paying Customers

### 1. Pre-Launch Activities (Month 0-1)

#### A. Community Building
**Objective**: Create buzz before launch

**Tactics**:
- **Landing page with waitlist** (Thai/English)
  - Promise: "First 1,000 users get lifetime 50% discount"
  - Goal: 2,000 waitlist signups

- **Instagram/TikTok teaser content**
  - Behind-the-scenes: AI recognizing Thai food
  - "Can AI identify Som Tam?" challenge videos
  - Nutrition myth-busting (Thai food edition)

- **Influencer seeding** (Pre-launch partnerships)
  - 10 micro-influencers (10K-50K followers)
  - Health/fitness/food content creators
  - Provide beta access

- **PR preparation**
  - Press kit: "Thai startup brings AI nutrition to Thai food"
  - Pitch to tech media: TechCrunch, TechinAsia, e27
  - Local media: Bangkok Post, The Nation, ThaiPBS

**Budget**: THB 200,000
**Timeline**: 4 weeks before launch

#### B. Beta Testing Program
**Objective**: Validate product, gather testimonials

**Tactics**:
- Recruit 100 beta testers from waitlist
- Structured feedback surveys
- Weekly product iterations
- Incentivize reviews (lifetime discount)

**Deliverable**: 20+ testimonials, 10+ case studies

### 2. Launch Week (Month 1, Week 1)

#### Launch Day Strategy

**Morning**:
- Product Hunt launch (global tech community)
- Press release distribution
- Influencer content goes live (10 posts scheduled)

**Afternoon**:
- Facebook/Instagram ads begin
- TikTok ads begin
- Google Play + App Store featured application submitted

**Evening**:
- Community celebration livestream
- Founder Q&A on Instagram Live
- Early adopter thank you gifts

#### Launch Channels

**Owned Media**:
- Instagram post (3 carousel formats)
- TikTok video (3 variations)
- Facebook post (community groups)
- LINE Official Account broadcast
- Email to waitlist (2,000 people)

**Earned Media**:
- PR coverage (Tech blogs)
- Influencer posts (10 creators)
- Beta tester social shares
- Product Hunt community

**Paid Media** (THB 100,000 launch week):
- Instagram ads: THB 40,000
- TikTok ads: THB 40,000
- Facebook ads: THB 20,000

**Goal**: 2,000 downloads in launch week

### 3. Growth Channels (Months 1-6)

#### Channel 1: Content Marketing (PRIMARY)
**Investment**: 40% of marketing budget
**Target CAC**: THB 100-200

**Content Pillars**:

1. **Educational Content** (Build authority)
   - "Truth about Thai food nutrition" series
   - "Blood sugar impact of common Thai dishes"
   - "Eating sequence guide for Thai meals"
   - Blog posts, infographics, short videos

2. **Entertaining Content** (Viral potential)
   - "AI tries Thai street food" challenges
   - "Healthy vs Unhealthy Thai food swaps"
   - Before/after nutrition makeovers
   - User transformation stories

3. **Useful Tools** (Lead generation)
   - Free "Thai Food Glycemic Index Guide"
   - "30 Healthy Thai Recipes" ebook
   - "Blood Sugar Management Checklist"
   - Email capture for downloads

**Distribution**:
- Instagram: 3 posts/week + 5 stories/day
- TikTok: 5 videos/week
- Blog/SEO: 2 long-form posts/week
- YouTube: 1 video/week
- LINE Official: 3 broadcasts/week

**Team**: 1 content creator + 1 designer

#### Channel 2: Influencer Partnerships (PRIMARY)
**Investment**: 30% of marketing budget
**Target CAC**: THB 300-500

**Tier Structure**:

**Micro-Influencers** (10K-50K followers)
- **Number**: 20 creators/month
- **Budget**: THB 5,000-10,000 per post
- **Content**: Authentic app reviews, tutorials
- **ROI**: Highest engagement rates

**Mid-Tier Influencers** (50K-200K followers)
- **Number**: 5 creators/month
- **Budget**: THB 20,000-50,000 per post
- **Content**: Campaign collaborations
- **ROI**: Broader reach

**Nutritionists & Fitness Trainers**
- **Number**: 10 professionals
- **Approach**: Free premium access, co-branded content
- **Value**: Credibility and trust

**Selection Criteria**:
- ✅ Engaged Thai-speaking audience
- ✅ Health/fitness/food niche
- ✅ Authentic content style (not overly polished)
- ✅ Good engagement rate (>3%)
- ❌ Promote junk food or fad diets

#### Channel 3: Paid Social (SCALING)
**Investment**: 20% of marketing budget
**Target CAC**: THB 400-800

**Platform Split**:
- Instagram: 50% (highest intent)
- TikTok: 35% (viral potential)
- Facebook: 15% (older demographic)

**Campaign Types**:

1. **Awareness Campaigns**
   - Video ads: "Meet your AI nutrition coach"
   - Problem-solution: "Confused about Thai food? We get it."
   - Target: Broad interest targeting
   - Goal: <THB 2 per video view

2. **Consideration Campaigns**
   - Carousel ads: Feature showcase
   - User testimonials: Real results
   - Target: Engaged with content
   - Goal: <THB 10 per link click

3. **Conversion Campaigns**
   - App install ads
   - Free trial prompts
   - Target: Retargeting + lookalikes
   - Goal: <THB 600 per install

**Creative Testing**:
- Run 5 ad variations per campaign
- Test weekly, kill losers, scale winners
- Focus on short-form video (performs best)

#### Channel 4: SEO & ASO (LONG-TERM)
**Investment**: 5% of marketing budget (mostly time)
**Target CAC**: THB 50-150

**SEO Strategy**:
- **Keywords**: "Thai food nutrition", "blood sugar Thai food", "healthy Thai recipes"
- **Content**: Long-form guides (2,000+ words)
- **Backlinks**: Partner with nutrition blogs, food sites
- **Goal**: Rank page 1 for 20 key terms by Month 6

**ASO Strategy**:
- **App Store Keywords**: nutrition, Thai food, health, AI coach
- **Screenshots**: Feature Thai food recognition
- **Reviews**: Encourage happy users to review (in-app prompt)
- **Goal**: Top 10 in "Health & Fitness" category Thailand

#### Channel 5: Referral Program (VIRAL LOOP)
**Investment**: 5% of marketing budget (credits/rewards)
**Target CAC**: THB 0-100 (cheapest channel)

**Mechanics**:
- **Give**: Referrer gets THB 100 credit
- **Get**: Referee gets THB 100 credit (when subscribes)
- **Bonus**: After 5 referrals, get 1 month free premium

**Promotion**:
- In-app sharing flow (WhatsApp, LINE, Instagram)
- Unique referral codes
- Leaderboard: "Top referrers this month"
- Prizes for top 10 referrers

**Goal**: 20% of new users from referrals by Month 6

## Phase 2: Thailand Expansion (Months 6-18)

6. **Pattaya** (M13-15): Tourism, expat population

**Regional Strategy**:
- Partner with local fitness influencers
- Regional food specialization (Isaan, Southern Thai)
- Local university ambassador programs
- Regional press coverage

### 2. Scaling Growth Channels

#### Content Marketing Expansion
- **Team Growth**: 3 content creators, 2 designers, 1 video editor
- **Output**: 5 Instagram posts/week, 10 TikToks/week, 2 YouTube videos/week
- **SEO**: 100+ ranking keywords, 50K monthly organic visitors
- **Goal**: Content = 30% of new users

#### Influencer Scale
- **Macro-influencers**: 2-3 campaigns with 500K+ followers
- **Micro-influencer program**: 50 active creators monthly
- **Ambassador program**: 100 unpaid advocates (lifetime premium)
- **Budget**: THB 500K/month

#### Paid Media Scale
- **Budget**: THB 400K/month
- **Focus**: TikTok (70%), Instagram (30%)
- **Creative**: UGC-style ads (user testimonials)
- **Target CAC**: Maintain <THB 400

### 3. Product-Led Growth Enhancements

#### Viral Features Addition
1. **Social Sharing**: "Share your meal score" to Instagram stories
2. **Challenges**: "30-Day Blood Sugar Challenge" with leaderboards
3. **Meal Comparison**: "Your pad Thai vs healthier alternative"
4. **Progress Badges**: Shareable achievements

#### Onboarding Optimization
- Reduce time-to-value: First scan in <60 seconds
- Personalization quiz: Health goals, dietary preferences
- Quick wins: Immediate actionable insight from first scan

#### Retention Features
- **Push notifications**: Personalized meal reminders
- **Email sequences**: Educational content series
- **In-app engagement**: Daily tips, challenges, streaks

### 4. B2B Pilot Program Launch (Month 12+)

**Objective**: Land 10 corporate clients by Month 18

**Target Profile**:
- 100-500 employees
- Bangkok-based
- Progressive HR culture
- Budget for wellness

**Sales Approach**:
1. **Inbound leads**: Whitepaper "ROI of Corporate Wellness"
2. **Outbound**: LinkedIn outreach to HR directors
3. **Partnerships**: Broker connections (insurance companies)
4. **Pilots**: 3-month free trial for 50 employees

**Sales Cycle**: 3-6 months
**Team**: 1 B2B sales rep (hire Month 10)

## Phase 3: SEA Expansion (Months 18-36)

### Target: 500,000 MAU | 27,500 Paying Customers

### 1. Market Selection & Entry

**Priority 1: Malaysia** (Month 18-24)
- **Why**: Cultural similarity (Muslim population, similar food)
- **Population**: 33 million (Thai diaspora present)
- **Adaptation**: Add Malay language, halal certification focus
- **Food Database**: Malay cuisine (700+ recipes)
- **Target**: 50,000 MAU by Month 24

**Priority 2: Singapore** (Month 24-30)
- **Why**: Affluent, tech-savvy, health-conscious
- **Population**: 5.9 million, high smartphone penetration
- **Adaptation**: Premium pricing (higher willingness-to-pay)
- **Food Database**: Singaporean hawker food (500+ items)
- **Target**: 30,000 MAU by Month 30

**Priority 3: Indonesia** (Month 30-36)
- **Why**: Largest SEA population (280 million)
- **Challenge**: Diverse, complex market
- **Adaptation**: Bahasa Indonesia, regional cuisines
- **Food Database**: Indonesian cuisine (1,000+ recipes)
- **Target**: 100,000 MAU by Month 36

### 2. Localization Strategy

**Per Market Requirements**:
- Language support (native + English)
- Local food database (500-1,000 items)
- Local payment methods
- Cultural adaptation (religious dietary rules)
- Local influencer partnerships
- Country-specific App Store presence

### 3. Regional Brand Building

**Pan-SEA Positioning**: "Southeast Asia's AI Nutrition Coach"

**Regional Marketing**:
- Regional food challenges (best dishes across SEA)
- Cross-country user competitions
- SEA health statistics and content
- Regional partnerships (Grab, Foodpanda)

## Sales Strategy

### B2C Sales (Self-Serve)

**In-App Conversion Flow**:
1. **Free Tier**: Use product, hit limitations
2. **Paywall Moment**: "Unlock full 8-dimension analysis"
3. **Trial Offer**: "7-day free trial, then THB 199/month"
4. **Upgrade**: Frictionless in-app purchase
5. **Onboarding**: Premium feature tour

**Optimization Points**:
- Paywall timing (after 3rd scan vs 10th scan)
- Trial vs no trial
- Pricing page design
- Purchase flow UX

### B2B Sales (High-Touch)

**Sales Process**:

**Stage 1: Prospecting**
- LinkedIn outreach
- Warm introductions
- Inbound from content
- Conference/event networking

**Stage 2: Discovery Call** (30 min)
- Understand wellness program goals
- Current solutions and pain points
- Decision makers and budget
- Success metrics

**Stage 3: Demo & Proposal** (45 min)
- Product demo (admin + employee view)
- Customization options
- Pricing proposal
- ROI modeling

**Stage 4: Pilot Agreement** (3 months)
- 50-100 employees
- Success metrics defined
- Regular check-ins
- Executive sponsorship

**Stage 5: Contract Negotiation**
- Full rollout pricing
- Contract terms (1-3 years)
- Implementation plan
- Support SLAs

**Stage 6: Implementation** (1-2 months)
- Bulk onboarding
- HR system integration
- Employee education
- Launch event

**Sales Team Structure** (Month 12+):
- 1 Head of B2B Sales
- 2 Account Executives (by Month 18)
- 1 Customer Success Manager (by Month 24)

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Product-Market Fit
**Indicators**: <15% D30 retention, high churn
**Mitigation**: Rapid iteration, user interviews, pivot features

### Risk 2: High CAC, Low Conversion
**Indicators**: CAC >THB 800, conversion <2%
**Mitigation**: Focus on organic/viral channels, improve onboarding

### Risk 3: Competitive Response
**Indicators**: MyFitnessPal adds Thai food, local competitor launches
**Mitigation**: Speed to market, brand building, community moat

### Risk 4: Regulatory Issues
**Indicators**: Medical device classification, health claims restrictions
**Mitigation**: Legal review, position as information tool, avoid claims

## Conclusion: GTM Playbook Summary

**Quarter 1**: Build → Launch Bangkok → Validate PMF
**Quarter 2-3**: Optimize → Scale Bangkok → Achieve 10K MAU
**Quarter 4-6**: Expand → All Thailand → Launch B2B → Reach 100K MAU
**Quarter 7-12**: Regional → Enter Malaysia/Singapore → 500K MAU

**Core GTM Philosophy**:
1. **Start narrow**: Own Thai nutrition before expanding
2. **Build community**: Users are advocates
3. **Content-first**: Education builds trust and traffic
4. **B2C2B motion**: Consumer traction enables enterprise
5. **Data-driven**: Test, measure, optimize constantly

Success requires disciplined execution, rapid iteration based on data, and maintaining laser focus on the Thai market before regional expansion.
