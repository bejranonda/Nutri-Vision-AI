## Research Objectives

### Primary Research Questions

1. **Pain Points**: What frustrates users about current nutrition tracking methods?
2. **Motivations**: Why do users want to track nutrition? What are their goals?
3. **Behaviors**: How do users currently manage their diet and health?
4. **Use Context**: When, where, and how do users interact with nutrition apps?
5. **Thai Food Challenges**: What specific challenges exist with Thai cuisine tracking?
6. **Feature Priorities**: Which features are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?
7. **Cultural Factors**: How do Thai cultural values influence nutrition app usage?

### Success Criteria

- **Sample Size**: 30-50 participants (qualitative) + 200+ survey responses (quantitative)
- **Diversity**: Mix of age groups, income levels, health goals, tech savviness
- **Insights**: Validate or invalidate 5 key assumptions about user needs
- **Actionable**: Research findings directly inform design decisions

## Research Methods

### Method 1: In-Depth Interviews (Qualitative)

**Format**: 1-on-1, moderated, 45-60 minutes

**Sample Size**: 20-30 participants (5-8 per segment)

**Location Options**:
- In-person: Coffee shops, co-working spaces (Bangkok)
- Remote: Zoom/Google Meet (other cities)

**Incentive**: THB 500 gift card or app premium subscription

**Key Activities**:
- Semi-structured interview (30 min)
- Show competitor apps for reaction (10 min)
- Show NutriVision wireframes for feedback (15 min)
- Q&A and wrap-up (5 min)

**Recording**: Audio/video with consent for analysis

---

### Method 2: Online Surveys (Quantitative)

**Format**: Self-administered, 10-15 minutes

**Sample Size**: 200-500 responses

**Distribution**:
- Facebook health groups
- Instagram stories (partner with micro-influencers)
- LINE Official Account
- University email lists
- Corporate wellness programs

**Incentive**: Enter to win 1 of 10 premium subscriptions (1 year)

**Key Topics**:
- Current nutrition tracking habits
- App usage and preferences
- Feature prioritization
- Willingness to pay
- Demographics and health goals

---

### Method 3: Diary Study (Contextual)

**Format**: 7-day food and app usage diary

**Sample Size**: 10-15 participants

**Method**:
- Daily photo diary of meals
- Daily journal entry (LINE chat or form)
- Note frustrations with current tracking methods
- Record time spent on nutrition-related activities

**Incentive**: THB 1,000 or premium subscription

**Key Insights**:
- Real-world behaviors (not self-reported ideals)
- Pain points in natural context
- Usage patterns throughout the week
- Meal types and timing

---

### Method 4: Competitive App Analysis (Observational)

**Format**: Participants use competitor apps for tasks

**Sample Size**: 10 participants

**Method**:
- Task 1: Log a Thai meal (Pad Thai) in MyFitnessPal
- Task 2: Scan a food item in Yazio
- Task 3: Find a Thai recipe in any nutrition app
- Observe struggles, time to complete, frustration points

**Recording**: Screen recording + think-aloud protocol

**Key Insights**:
- Usability issues with competitors
- Unmet needs and frustrations
- Features users like/dislike
- Opportunities for NutriVision

---

### Method 5: Card Sorting (Information Architecture)

**Format**: Open card sort for feature organization

**Sample Size**: 15-20 participants

**Method**:
- Provide 30-40 feature cards
- Ask participants to group related features
- Ask them to name each group
- Identify which group should be most prominent

**Platform**: Optimal Workshop or physical cards

**Key Insights**:
- Mental models for app organization
- Feature grouping preferences
- Navigation structure validation
- Priority features identification

- What are your hobbies?

**General Health & Nutrition**

1. How would you describe your current health? (Scale 1-10, why?)
2. Do you currently track or pay attention to what you eat? Why or why not?
3. What are your main health or fitness goals right now?
4. Have you tried any diets or eating plans? (Keto, intermittent fasting, etc.)

## Usability Testing Protocol

### Pre-Test Preparation

**Participants**: 10-20 users per testing round

**Materials Needed**:
- Prototype (low-fidelity or high-fidelity)
- Recording equipment (screen + audio)
- Test script
- Consent forms
- Incentive (THB 500 or app credit)

**Test Environment**:
- Quiet room (in-person) or video call (remote)
- Participant's own smartphone when possible
- Stable internet connection
- Test facilitator + note-taker

---

### Test Structure (60 minutes)

**1. Welcome & Introduction (5 min)**
- Thank participant
- Explain purpose and process
- Get consent for recording
- Assure no wrong answers
- Encourage think-aloud

**2. Pre-Test Questions (5 min)**
- Current nutrition tracking habits
- Smartphone usage comfort
- Health goals

**3. Task-Based Testing (35 min)**

**Task 1: Onboarding (5 min)**
- "Imagine you just downloaded this app. Walk me through setting up your account."
- Observe: Time to complete, confusion points, skip rate

**Task 2: Food Scanning (10 min)**
- "You just ate Pad Thai for lunch. Show me how you would log it."
- Observe: Can they find camera, understand results, confusion with AI

**Task 3: Recipe Discovery (5 min)**
- "You want to cook something healthy for dinner tonight. Find a Thai recipe."
- Observe: Navigation, filter usage, recipe selection

**Task 4: Check Progress (5 min)**
- "It's the end of the day. Check your nutrition scores and progress."
- Observe: Dashboard understanding, chart interpretation

**Task 5: Ask AI Coach (5 min)**
- "You're curious about blood sugar. Ask the AI nutritionist for advice."
- Observe: Chat interface, AI response satisfaction

**Task 6: Upgrade to Premium (5 min)**
- "You want to access all features. Find the subscription options."
- Observe: Can they find paywall, pricing clarity, payment flow

**4. Post-Test Questions (10 min)**
- Overall impression (1-10 scale)
- What did you like most?
- What was most confusing?
- Would you use this app regularly?
- Would you recommend to friends?
- Any missing features?

**5. Closing (5 min)**
- Thank you
- Provide incentive
- Invite to beta testing

---

### Metrics to Track

**Efficiency:**
- Task completion time
- Number of errors
- Number of clicks/taps to complete

**Effectiveness:**
- Task completion rate (% who complete successfully)
- Error rate
- Number of times help/instructions needed

**Satisfaction:**
- System Usability Scale (SUS) score
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Qualitative feedback themes

**Specific KPIs:**
- % who complete onboarding: Target >60%
- Time to first scan: Target <60 seconds
- % who understand 8-dimension scores: Target >70%
- % who would pay for premium: Target >30%
- SUS score: Target >75 (ideally >80)

## Analysis Framework

### Qualitative Analysis (Interviews & Usability Tests)

**Thematic Coding:**
1. Transcribe recordings
2. Code responses into themes (pain points, motivations, preferences)
3. Look for patterns across participants
4. Prioritize themes by frequency and impact

**Affinity Mapping:**
- Write each insight on sticky note
- Group related insights together
- Identify clusters and themes
- Create hierarchy of findings

**Key Questions:**
- What are the top 5 pain points?
- What are the top 5 motivators?
- What features are most desired?
- What cultural factors are most important?
- What would cause users to abandon the app?

---

### Quantitative Analysis (Surveys)

**Descriptive Statistics:**
- Calculate means, medians, modes
- Create frequency distributions
- Identify demographic patterns

**Cross-Tabulation:**
- Segment by age group, income, tech savviness
- Compare feature preferences across segments
- Analyze willingness to pay by demographics

**Prioritization:**
- Feature must-haves (>70% say must-have)
- Feature nice-to-haves (40-70%)
- Feature low-priority (<40%)

---
