## 1. Food Delivery Platform Partnerships

### A. GrabFood (Priority 1)

#### Company Profile
- **Market Share**: 35% of Thailand food delivery
- **Parent**: Grab Holdings (Singapore-based super app)
- **Users**: Millions across Southeast Asia
- **Revenue Model**: Commission-based (20-35% from restaurants)

#### Partnership Opportunity

**"Healthy Choice" Integration**

**Value Proposition to Grab**:
- Differentiation: First food delivery app with AI nutrition analysis
- Higher AOV: Users ordering healthier, premium options
- User engagement: More time in app, nutrition-focused browsing
- Corporate wellness: B2B channel (companies subsidize healthy employee meals)

**Value Proposition to NutriVision**:
- Distribution: Access to millions of active users
- Context: Nutrition analysis at moment of ordering decision
- Revenue: Commission on "Healthy Choice" orders
- Data: Meal patterns, ordering behavior

**Integration Model**:
1. **In-App Camera**: User takes photo of menu item
2. **NutriVision API**: Analyzes nutrition in real-time
3. **Display**: Shows 8-dimension score within Grab app
4. **Recommendation**: Suggests healthier alternatives
5. **Order**: User proceeds with informed choice

**Monetization**:
- **Option A**: Rev share on "Healthy Choice" orders (2-5%)
- **Option B**: Subscription bundling (GrabFood Premium includes NutriVision)
- **Option C**: White-label enterprise (Grab licenses technology)

**Business Case for Grab**:
- Differentiation from Foodpanda, LINE MAN
- Appeal to health-conscious users (growing segment)
- Corporate wellness partnerships
- ESG/sustainability alignment

**Path to Partnership**:
1. **Month 3-6**: Build integration MVP, create pitch deck
2. **Month 6**: Reach out through warm intro (investor/advisor network)
3. **Month 7-9**: Pilot with 1,000 test users
4. **Month 10-12**: Negotiate terms, launch integration

**Existing Precedent**:
- Good Doctor Technology Thailand partnered with Grab for telemedicine
- Proves Grab open to health tech integrations

#### Success Metrics
- Pilot: 10%+ of test users enable nutrition analysis
- Rollout: 100K monthly nutrition scans through Grab
- Revenue: THB 2-5M annually from partnership

## Partnership Success Metrics

### Key Performance Indicators

**For Each Partnership, Track**:
1. **User Acquisition**: # users from partner channel
2. **CAC**: Cost per acquisition through partnership
3. **Activation**: % who complete first scan
4. **Retention**: D30, D90 retention vs. organic users
5. **Revenue**: Direct revenue from partnership
6. **LTV**: Lifetime value of partnership users

### Partnership ROI Threshold
- **Minimum**: Partnership users have equal or better LTV than paid channels
- **Target**: Partnership users have 1.5x+ LTV (due to trust/endorsement)
- **Ideal**: Negative CAC (partner pays us or cross-promotion only)

3. **Data Ownership**: User data rights and privacy
4. **IP Rights**: Technology licensing terms
5. **Term & Termination**: Duration, exit clauses
6. **Performance Metrics**: Commitments from both sides

## Conclusion: Partnership Strategy Summary

**Philosophy**: Partnerships amplify growth, but product quality comes first.

**Priorities**:
1. **Year 1**: Quick wins (LINE OA, integrations), relationship building
2. **Year 2**: Major partnerships (food delivery, insurance), B2B focus
3. **Year 3**: Ecosystem play (deep integrations), regional expansion

**Success Criteria**:
- 30%+ of users acquired through partnerships by Year 2
- 2-3 strategic partnerships generating THB 10M+ revenue annually by Year 3
- Partnership users have higher LTV than paid acquisition users

**Key Principle**: Choose partners that align with mission (health improvement) and provide either:
1. **Distribution**: Access to large user bases
2. **Credibility**: Trust and endorsement
3. **Revenue**: Direct monetization
4. **Data**: Insights to improve product

Strategic partnerships, executed well, can accelerate NutriVision AI's path to market leadership in Southeast Asia.
